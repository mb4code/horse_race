Lower Bound

Why the answer cannot be below 13

Step 1: How much information does one race give?
One 5-horse race can finish in 5! = 120 different orders.
Step 2: How many total rankings are possible?
The 25 horses could be arranged in 25! full rankings before any races happen.
Step 3: Compare outcomes to possibilities
After r races, you can distinguish at most 120^r cases. To identify one exact ordering, you need 120^r >= 25!.
Step 4: Solve for r
r >= log(25!) / log(120) = 12.1156..., so any perfect strategy needs at least 13 races.
What this means in the app
The simulator can search for strategies that hit 13 on specific hidden orders, but it should never claim fewer than 13 races are enough in the general case.
